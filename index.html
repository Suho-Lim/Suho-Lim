About

I'm an Electrical Engineer based in Denver, Colorado, working at the intersection of hardware design and machine learning.

My research focuses on deploying AI on resource-constrained edge devices — autonomous robots that sort waste, wearables that help visually impaired users navigate, and ear-worn sensors that monitor blood pressure continuously. I love the moment when a transfomer model structure quantize to small 8bit on not suported architecture.

Currently completing my MS in Electrical Engineering at CU Denver, with publications ACM MobiSys. Always curious about how AI, robotics, and medical systems can come together to make life better.

Research & projects

2024 — 2026
EchoVision — AI Navigation for the Visually Impaired
A wearable computer vision device that turns real-time object detection into haptic feedback. Hybrid NPU-CPU deployment of EfficientViT-SAM + YOLO on the Hailo-8 accelerator.
YOLO Hailo-8 PyTorch Linux
↗ 2024 — 2025
Automated Waste-Sorting Robotic System
Led the design of an autonomous metal-detection robot — custom YOLO models on Raspberry Pi for vision, TI Launchpad for low-level motor control, plus a multi-DOF robotic arm I designed and calibrated for precise pickup.
PyTorch Raspberry Pi TI Launchpad Robotics
↗ 2025 — 2026
eBP — Wearable Ear-Worn Blood Pressure Monitor -in developing
A non-invasive ear-based blood pressure wearable using PPG and pressure sensors. Built the data acquisition pipeline, synchronized sensor inputs with motor-driven ear-canal adjustments, and validated through 100-cycle stress testing.
